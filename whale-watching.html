About Whales

Whales are mammals that breathe air and nurse their young. Humpbacks are known for their spectacular breaching and complex songs that can travel for miles underwater.

Most whales in Cabo migrate from the cold waters of Alaska and Canada. They travel thousands of miles to the warm lagoons of Baja to mate and give birth.

The most common sightings are Humpback and Gray whales. Occasionally, visitors may spot Blue whales, Orcas, or Whale Sharks in the surrounding waters.

We strictly follow environmental guidelines to minimize impact. This includes maintaining safe distances, limiting observation time, and avoiding any disruption to their natural behavior.

We recommend sunscreen, a hat, polarized sunglasses, and a light jacket as it can get breezy on the water. Don't forget your camera!

Whale Watching in Cabo San Lucas

Every winter, thousands of humpback and blue whales migrate to the warm waters of the Sea of Cortez — and Cabo San Lucas sits right in their path. From December through April, our whale watching tours offer front-row seats to some of nature's most awe-inspiring spectacles.

Watch in wonder as these gentle giants breach, slap their tails, and sing — sometimes just meters from the boat. Our experienced naturalist crew knows exactly where to find them.
